Description

Diving (Yohannes, 2020)

Exploring the world through diving can be an enjoyable experience offering a unique perspective that land cannot provide. Whether you choose scuba diving or snorkeling, diving allows you to immerse yourself in the beauty of environments from vibrant coral reefs bustling with sea creatures to sunken vessels that hold tales of history. It's not just about seeking thrills; diving also provides a connection with nature. Drifting weightlessly in the water, you may encounter dolphins, sea turtles, and delicate yet stunning ecosystems. Dive training and certification programs are accessible globally, welcoming enthusiasts of all skill levels into this sport. Beyond being a hobby, diving presents opportunities for exploration, self-discovery, and personal development. Answer the call of the sea and unveil a whole new world beneath the ocean's surface!

Safety procedures

Before starting diving, thorough preparation before, during, and after the dive is essential to ensure safety and a memorable experience, including the following:

  • Diving gear inspection:

    Conduct a thorough check of all the equipment that will be used during the dive, such as the regulator, mask, snorkel, and fins. Pay special attention to the connection of the regulator to the cylinder. Regular and thorough maintenance of the equipment is necessary.

  • Medical considerations:

    Undergo a medical check before diving to ensure physical fitness. It is important to discuss any health issues related to diving with a doctor, including concerns about ears, sinuses, or allergies.

  • Listen to the briefing clearly:

    It is crucial to listen to the instructions from the instructor before diving at a dive site. This will help you understand the diving conditions better and know what actions to take if something happens underwater.

  • Follow the buddy system on every dive:

    Plan the dive with the buddy who will accompany you underwater. It is also vital to discuss the plan in case of separation underwater or other emergency procedures.

  • Plan the dive:

    Planning the dive is very important, such as the time spent underwater, the air limit in the cylinder, depth of the dive, and other plans. Plan a safety stop before ending the dive for three minutes or more.

  • Maintain neutral buoyancy:

    Adjust weight at the surface for neutral buoyancy. Maintain neutral buoyancy while underwater. Use appropriate weights to facilitate neutral buoyancy.

  • Breathing techniques:

    Breathe correctly while diving and never hold your breath when underwater.

  • Know the laws and regulations of diving regarding what is allowed and what is not.

Diving tips

Scuba diving tips for beginners include making a checklist of necessary items, bringing a dry bag to protect belongings from water damage, remembering to bring sunglasses and their case, and taking precautions to avoid sea sickness. It is important to choose the right dive center that caters to beginners and not let peer pressure affect your comfort level.

Experienced divers recommend wearing socks when putting on a wetsuit to make the process easier. Hydration is crucial to prevent decompression sickness, so bring an adequate supply of drinking water. Refreshing your skills with a course before diving again is wise, and beginners should always listen to professionals and ask questions when unsure.

Marine conservations

Marine conservation (3wdivemaster, 2022)

Marine conservation is the protection of marine species and ecosystems in oceans and seas worldwide. It involves the protection and restoration of marine species and populations. In the diving world, there are conservation divers, who provide hands-on training in marine conservation and the monitoring techniques used by scientists or professionals.
