Course options

Generally, there are many diving course options available for divers of different skill levels, specializations, or interest. However, the following are the common diving courses:

  • Open water diver:

    This beginner-level course teaches the basic principles of scuba diving and the necessary skills for open water diving at certain depths.

  • Advanced open water diver:

    The next level is the advanced open water. This course broadens the diver’s knowledge and skills, with specializations such as night diving.

  • Rescue diver:

    This course teaches how to manage risks and handle emergency situations underwater.

  • Divemaster:

    This is the first professional level in scuba diving, which delves deeper into diving theory and prepares divers to assist in teaching others.

  • Specializations:

    These courses allow divers to focus on specific skills such as underwater photography, nitrox, cave diving, and many more.

  • Instructor Development Course (IDC):

    This course is aimed at divemasters or those at an equivalent level who wish to become dive instructor.

Training schools

There are more than 160 scuba diving schools worldwide. However, here are the top 7 dive training schools in the world, such as PADI (Professional Association of Diving Instructors), CMAS (Confédération Mondiale des Activités Subaquatiques), SSI (Scuba Schools International), BSAC (British Sub-Aqua Club), SDI (Scuba Diving International), NAUI (The National Association of Underwater Instructors), and RAID (The Rebreather Association of International Divers).
